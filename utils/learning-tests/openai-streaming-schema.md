## Structural Differences

### With include_usage: true
- All content chunks have `"usage": null`
- Final chunk before [DONE] has `"choices": []` and `"usage": {...}`

### Without include_usage (or false)
- Content chunks do NOT have `"usage"` field at all
- No usage chunk at end

## Observed Behavior

### usage field in content chunks

- **Direct (no stream_options)**: usage field ABSENT
- **Direct (include_usage: true)**: usage field present (null)
- **Direct (include_usage: false)**: usage field ABSENT

## Key Finding: Structural Difference

**This is NOT just about the final usage chunk.**

When `include_usage: true`, OpenAI changes the structure of EVERY chunk:

| Scenario | Content chunk structure | Extra usage chunk |
|----------|------------------------|-------------------|
| No stream_options | No `usage` field | No |
| include_usage: false | No `usage` field | No |
| include_usage: true | `"usage": null` in every chunk | Yes |

### Implication for ai-proxy

Since ai-proxy forces `include_usage: true`:

1. **Every chunk** sent to client has `"usage": null` field
2. **Extra chunk** at end with `choices: []` and usage data
3. **Different byte count** - responses are larger
4. **Parsing differences** - clients may not expect `usage` field

### Sample chunk comparison

**Without include_usage (direct to OpenAI):**


**With include_usage (via ai-proxy):**
